Formant-Guided Speech Repair for Enhanced Comprehension of Mandarin Dysarthric Speech Demo

Abstract

Dysarthria severely degrades speech intelligibility, limiting effective communication and social participation. While recent ASR-based approaches improve recognition accuracy, they discard prosodic cues and cannot restore natural speech, restricting usability in daily conversations. We propose a speech-in, speech-out repair framework for dysarthric speech, featuring a Formant-Corrective Spectral Warping (FCSW) module that corrects vowel-space distortions. The system then integrates a dysarthric-adapted ASR with speaker-adaptive TTS, enabling speech reconstruction that significantly improves listener comprehension while preserving speaker identity. Compared to the source dysarthric speech, our proposed repair system reduces character error rate (CER) by 72.4%, improves subjective MOS Intelligibility, Comprehension, and Fluency by over 90%, and decreases listening effort by 61.6%. Vowel articulation features (VSA) are substantially restored, with expansions of 249.5% for males and 62.8% for females, confirming improved articulation.

We present speech repair results for dysarthric speakers. Each sentence is provided in seven versions: Original, Pure TTS, ASR-TTS, +Speaker Adaption, +FCSW, Our Proposed, and DiffDSR-inspired Baseline.
